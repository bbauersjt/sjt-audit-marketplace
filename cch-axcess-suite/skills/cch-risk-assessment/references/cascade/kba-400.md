# KBA-400 — Scoping & Mapping of Significant Accounts, Transactions & Disclosures

**Form:** KBA-400 (`dataBindingKey` KBA400). Uniform across the five non-SA titles.
**Role in the cascade:** the scoping hinge. It pulls the area set from AUD-100, you make the scoping determinations, CCH recommends the audit programs, and then a **per-area section at the bottom** is where you **select the relevant assertions** for each significant area. Those relevant assertions are what populate KBA-502's risk grid.

This skill decides the scoping and the assertion selection. `cch-axcess` parses the live form and writes it. KBA-400 is a grid/scoping-heavy form — **read it live first**; some of its add-row grids and the custom-multiselect (`CtrlConTestWp`/C09) need per-form handling on the mechanics side.

## Inputs

- AUD-100 area selection (already on the engagement).
- Intake scope sheet: significant Y/N per area + basis (`references/intake.md`, `scoping/significance.md`).
- Recommended-program map (`scoping/area-map-by-title.md`).

## Progressive disclosure (why this is multi-pass)

KBA-400's per-area assertion section does not exist in the form until the scoping selections are answered — those rows are cascade-hidden objects whose visibility flips on as the driver answers post. So the order is forced: answer the scoping determinations first, let `cch-axcess` re-snapshot, *then* the per-area assertion blocks appear to be filled. Don't expect to see (or write) the assertion section on the first read. `cch-axcess` runs the snapshot → answer-drivers → re-snapshot → diff loop to a fixed point; this skill just supplies the answers for each pass.

## Step 1 — Scoping determinations ("the 6 options") — VERIFIED 2026-05-30

The scoping grid is the collection **`.KBA400.Scoping`**, one row per audit area (16 areas on the NFP title captured). Each row has **six X/dash toggles** — these are "the 6 options to click." valueKey `NOTDASH` = X (applies), `DASH` = does not:

| # | Property key | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `SigClassTans` | Significant Class of Transactions |
| 2 | `MaterialAccount` | Material / Significant Account |
| 3 | `SigDisclosure` | Significant Disclosure |
| 4 | `SigAcctEst` | Significant Accounting Estimate |
| 5 | `SigFraudRisk` | Significant Fraud Risk |
| 6 | `substantivetesting` | Substantive testing indicator |

Plus per row: `Name` (area label, read-only), `FurtherUnderstanding` (recommended-answer dropdown — e.g. Cash = "Understand Process and Related Controls", valueKey `SCOPINGRECOMMENDEDANSWER`), `CtrlConTestWp` (controls-testing WP multiselect — e.g. Cash = `KBA-403;KBA-407`), `Comments` (free text).

**Judgment:** for each area, set the toggles to X per the significance call (`scoping/significance.md`) — SigClassTans/MaterialAccount for quantitatively significant balances/flows; SigDisclosure/SigAcctEst/SigFraudRisk per the qualitative tests. An area with all six dashed is out of scope. (Verified example: Cash = SigClassTans X, rest dash.) The empty grids `.KBA400.ReconciliationProcess`, `.RiskRelatedSpecificrisks`, `.IdentifiedItems`, `.SigDisclosures` are addable lists — fill only if the engagement has those items.

## Step 2 — Confirm CCH's recommended programs against your scope

Once scoping is set, CCH recommends the audit programs (the forms). **Reconcile against your significance call:**
- CCH recommends a program for an area you scoped **out** → de-select it (or don't add it) and document why it's immaterial.
- CCH misses an area you scoped **in** → add it manually.
- Your significance determination (`scoping/significance.md`) governs; CCH's recommendation is a starting point. Note any deviation.

The resulting program set = the AUD-8xx forms to add, resolved by area+title via `scoping/area-map-by-title.md` and handed to `cch-axcess` (`add-audit-programs.md`).

## Step 3 — Select relevant assertions per significant area (the core judgment)

The bottom section is the collection **`.KBA400.AuditareaRelevantAssertions`** (VERIFIED 2026-05-30): one row cascades in per scoped area, with properties **`AuditAreaName`**, **`Assertion`** (multiselect of the six), and **`comment`**. For each scoped area, set `Assertion` to the **relevant assertions** — those for which there's a reasonable possibility of a material misstatement. This selection is what drives the per-assertion grid on the AUD-8xx programs / KBA-502 rollup. (Note: this is its own collection — it is NOT `.{AREA}.RelevantAssertion`, which is the program form's IR/CR grid.)

**Default rule:** for each significant area, select **all CCH-applicable assertions** (the six minus the area's N/A assertions), then de-select only an assertion with genuinely no exposure (rare — be conservative; an unselected assertion gets no risk assessment and no testing).

**N/A assertions by area** (CCH server-side — no checkbox exists; don't try to select):

| Area | N/A assertion(s) |
|---|---|
| Cash (CASH) | AV |
| Investments (INVEST) | CU |
| Property & Equipment (PPE) | CU |
| Accounts Payable (AP), Other Liabilities (OL), Equity (EQUITY) | RO |

(Capture more as each area is built — see `programs/_conventions.md`.)

**Which assertions are typically the *elevated* ones** (drives where IR goes above LOW on KBA-502) — read from `defaults/{CODE}.md` for the area + audit type. Relevance (selected Y/N) and risk level (IR) are separate calls: select the assertion if a material misstatement is reasonably possible; set its IR level separately on KBA-502.

**Assertion relevance cues:**
- **EO** — overstatement-prone assets (cash, AR, investments, PPE), revenue occurrence.
- **CO** — understatement-prone liabilities (AP, accruals, debt), revenue/contribution completeness.
- **RO** — ownership/restriction questions (investments, PPE held vs leased, restricted net assets/cash).
- **AV** — anything with an estimate or valuation (allowances, fair value, depreciation, impairment, accruals).
- **CU** — period-end cutoff exposure (revenue, purchases, payroll, contributions near year-end).
- **UC** — disclosure-heavy areas (debt covenants, restrictions, related parties, commitments, fair-value leveling, net-asset classes).
