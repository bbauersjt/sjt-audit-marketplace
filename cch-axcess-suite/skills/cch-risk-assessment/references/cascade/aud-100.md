# AUD-100 — Engagement-Level Tailoring Questions

**Form:** AUD-100 (`dataBindingKey` AUD100; AUD100S for Single Audit). Uniform across the five non-SA titles.
**Role in the cascade:** the first driving form. Its answers (a) select the **major audit areas** in play and (b) decide **whether and where we test controls**. KBA-400 reads the area selection from here; the controls decision flows to KBA-502 (whether CR can drop below MAX) and to KBA-401/403 (controls work).

This skill decides the answers. `cch-axcess` (`fill-kc-form.md`) parses the live form and writes them — including its fixed-point fill that catches gated follow-up questions. **Read the live form first** to get current option lists; AUD-100 re-versions yearly and options are gated.

## Inputs

From intake (`references/intake.md`): the entity's areas in play, the significant-area list, and the controls posture (does the firm intend to rely on controls this year?). Plus prior-year AUD-100 if rolling forward.

## What AUD-100 controls

| Driver | What it sets | This skill's judgment |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement/entity tailoring (Y/N) | Reporting framework, prior-period, group audit, specialist use, etc. | Answer from the entity facts + prior FS. |
| **Audit areas multiselect** | The set of FS areas that exist for this engagement | Select every area from intake's area list (`scoping/area-map-by-title.md`). This gates which programs are even available. |
| Operating-effectiveness / controls intent | Whether the firm will test operating effectiveness of controls | Default **No** (substantive approach) unless the firm plans controls reliance — see decision below. |
| **Controls-testing areas multiselect** (`OperatingAuditAreas`) | Which areas get controls testing | Populated only after audit areas + operating-effectiveness are set (gated). Select the areas where controls will be tested — usually empty on a substantive audit. |

> The `OperatingAuditAreas` multiselect is **gated**: its options don't populate until the audit-area and operating-effectiveness answers are in. A single fill pass misses it — `cch-axcess` loops to a fixed point. Don't assume an empty option list means "nothing to choose"; re-read after the upstream answers post.

## The controls-testing decision (the important one)

This is the call that determines whether CR can come off MAX anywhere on KBA-502.

1. **Default: substantive approach.** Most of the firm's audits don't test operating effectiveness — set operating-effectiveness **No**, leave `OperatingAuditAreas` empty, and CR stays **MAX** everywhere (KBA-502). This is the right default for small/closely-held entities, most NPO/HOA, and any engagement where controls reliance isn't planned.
2. **Test controls only when all hold:** controls are designed and implemented (KBA-401/403 support it), reliance is efficient (high transaction volume where substantive testing alone is costly), and the firm intends to take the credit. Then set operating-effectiveness **Yes** and select the specific areas in `OperatingAuditAreas`.
3. **Required by engagement type:** governmental Single Audit requires controls work over compliance (but that's the `*S`/major-program flow, outside this form's substantive scope). For EBP full-scope, consider controls over participant data and contributions.
4. **Document the basis** — the controls decision is a reviewer hot spot. Note why in the engagement memory / KBA-400 comment.

If unsure whether the firm wants controls reliance this year, **ask** rather than defaulting silently — it changes the entire downstream approach.

## Roll-forward

If prior-year AUD-100 exists, default to its answers, then challenge: new areas this year (add), areas gone (remove), any change in controls intent. Never carry the controls decision forward without confirming it still holds.
