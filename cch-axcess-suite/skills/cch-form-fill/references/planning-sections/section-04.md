# Section 04 — Internal Controls

**Forms:** KBA-401 (Entity-Level Controls), KBA-402 (General IT Controls), KBA-403/405/406/407/408 (Activity-Level Controls — cash receipts / disbursements / payroll / treasury / reporting-closing).

**Rule: the five cycle forms hold far more latent fields than render** — they are cascade-gated and collapse under a substantive approach. Never fill by latent size; only the live set matters.

---

## What you need to know to answer Section 04

### A. Control approach — the master gate for this whole section
- **Will you test operating effectiveness of controls?** (= Engagement Profile toggle.) Firm default **No — substantive, control risk = max.**
- Per cycle: **"Will substantive procedures alone provide sufficient appropriate audit evidence?"** → **Yes** under the substantive approach → operating-effectiveness **testing collapses to N/A**; you document understanding/walkthrough only.
- **Exception:** where a cycle relies on controls (e.g., current-year procurement with control rows Yes/Yes/Yes), that cycle gets tested. Any controls-reliance decision is the override to capture.

### B. Entity-level controls narrative (KBA-401) — COSO
Five components (Control Environment, Risk Assessment, Information & Communication, Control Activities, Monitoring) × their principles/points of focus. Per component: **memo-format toggle** → one narrative instead of the principle-by-principle grid.
- Default: each principle **present & functioning, no deficiency.**
- Knowledge to gather: the control-environment narrative (carried forward / from firm policies + entity policies) and **any known control deficiencies** (default none → feed KBA-103).

### C. IT controls narrative (KBA-402)
- **IT environment facts:** key financial applications (the accounting/ERP system), databases, networks, operating systems; complexity; system changes/new implementations; data sharing; e-commerce. *(Entity fact — from Section 01 system info + IT understanding.)*
- ITGC objectives (acquire/develop, change management, service levels, security, third-party): default present/standard or memo. Most government entities = simple IT environment, high-level documentation.
- Knowledge: the IT environment description + any IT deficiencies (default none).

### D. Activity-level controls per cycle (KBA-403/405/406/407/408)
Only for cycles in scope. Each is a **walkthrough**: subprocesses included; who performs the process/controls; segregation of duties; and the confirmations — understand procedures, aware of deviations, recognize/resolve errors, **process implemented & operated consistently through the period**.
- Default: process documented, **SoD adequate, operating as documented, no deviations/deficiencies.**
- Knowledge to gather: the **process/walkthrough narrative per cycle** (who does what — often carried forward) + the controls-reliance decision from gate A.

---

## How Section 04 collapses
Under the substantive default, the section is: COSO narrative (memo) + IT narrative (memo) + a short walkthrough per cycle, all defaulting to "present/adequate/no deficiencies." The thousands of latent boxes never render. The genuine inputs are: **control approach** (A), **process owners / walkthrough narratives per cycle** (D), **the accounting/IT system** (C), and **any actual control deficiencies** (default none).

## Constants & cross-links
Process owners (D) overlap management contacts. Control deficiencies (default none) → **KBA-103** (concluding). Controls-reliance decisions tie back to the Engagement Profile and to the control-risk in KBA-502.
