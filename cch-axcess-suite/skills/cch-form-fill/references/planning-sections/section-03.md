# Section 03 — Understanding the Entity

**Forms:** KBA-302 (Understanding the Entity & Environment), KBA-303 (Fraud/Noncompliance Inquiries), AID-837 (Governance Minutes Review). Risk-factor outputs feed the risk-assessment process (KBA-502/503).

Live counts: KBA-302 = **690 fillable** (222 selects + 468 texts, in ~7 categories), KBA-303 = 117, AID-837 = 12. KBA-302's size is an illusion — the **memo-format toggle** (see below) collapses each category's line-items to one narrative.

## What you need to know to answer Section 03

### A. Entity-understanding narrative (KBA-302) — by category
A structured understanding across ~7 categories, **mostly carried forward from the prior file and updated** from current FS / GL / minutes. Each category has a *"document in memorandum format rather than individual line items?"* toggle → default **Yes** = write one narrative, skip the individual line prompts.

Categories and what to know:
- **Operations / nature** — type of government, services, revenue sources (taxes / charges / intergovernmental), major customers/taxpayers, locations, size & complexity.
- **Organizational structure & key management personnel.**
- **Governance** — structure, audit committee/equivalent, TCWG oversight of financial reporting.
- **Regulatory environment** — framework, new legislation/regulations affecting operations, any regulatory inquiries.
- **External / economic factors** — economic conditions, interest rates & financing availability, legal debt limits, inflation.
- **Investments** — complex/derivative instruments, significant new investments or dispositions.
- **Embedded exposure items (also feed other forms):** going-concern & liquidity indicators · **related-party listing** · pending/threatened litigation & contingencies · environmental/ARO liabilities · significant unusual transactions · IT/cybersecurity · concentrations/constraints (GASB 102) · net pension/OPEB liabilities.

Flags: related parties → **AUD-814**; going concern → going-concern eval; litigation → contingencies / lawyer letters; anything elevated → risk-assessment (KBA-502).

### B. Fraud & noncompliance inquiries (KBA-303)
- **Who was interviewed** — name(s), title(s), date. *(Engagement constants — management names. The same inquiry set is often run for management, governance, and internal audit; carry one set across interviewees.)*
- Internal audit function? *(profile constant.)*
- **Management's responses** to the "are you aware of…" inquiries (fraud, allegations, unusual transactions, JE/override irregularities, tips/complaints, related parties, noncompliance, cybersecurity incidents, concentrations) — **default "none / not aware."** Process questions (assess fraud risk, communicate ethics, report to governance) — **default Yes.** These are management's actual answers — present as default, confirm.

### C. Governance minutes review (AID-837)
A 6-step procedure checklist (read minutes, obtain written reps, review related contracts/agreements, discuss matters, modify procedures as needed, inquire where minutes unavailable). Generic responses + WP ref to the minutes-review workpaper. Named minutes = the governing body's (e.g., General Assembly) — constant. Nothing new beyond confirming minutes obtained through the report date.

## Constants surfaced here (→ master list)
Management names/titles + inquiry date (B); governing-body / minutes name (C). All already in the master set.

## Collapse lever — memorandum-format toggle
Several understanding/controls forms (KBA-302 here; KBA-401/403/etc. in Section 04) offer "document in a memorandum rather than line-by-line." Choosing memo collapses a whole category's sel/txt grid into one narrative box. Default to memo format where the firm does — this is what turns KBA-302's 690 boxes into ~7 narratives.
