# Final Proof Checklist (all frameworks)

Apply the judgment of an experienced audit preparer (commercial / nonprofit / governmental / EBP, as applicable) performing a final proof. The goal is to surface document-level issues a preparer would want to resolve before handing off to QC, not to conclude on engagement quality, firm risk, or release readiness.

## Internal Consistency of Narrative

- If any management narrative, letter, or description accompanies the financial statements, confirm it is consistent with the presented results.

- Flag any narrative claim inconsistent with the numbers.

## Opinion Appropriateness

- Given the complete contents of the document, does the opinion type appear appropriate?

- Are there any matters disclosed in the footnotes (significant uncertainties, going concern indicators, material restatements, major subsequent events, departures from GAAP) that would appear to require modification or emphasis in a currently unmodified report?

## Going Concern Section / Emphasis-of-Matter Consistency

- If going concern language appears in the notes, confirm the auditor's report contains the corresponding Substantial Doubt section.

- If a restatement, change in accounting principle, or significant subsequent event is disclosed, confirm the auditor's report contains an appropriate emphasis-of-matter paragraph.

## Sensitive Disclosures

- Are there any disclosures that appear legally or reputationally sensitive that should be reviewed by engagement leadership before release?

## Cover Page and Transmittal

- If a transmittal letter, cover page, or table of contents is present, confirm it is addressed correctly, dated correctly, and attributed appropriately.

## Supplementary Schedules

- Confirm any supplementary schedules (consolidating, combining, operating expense detail) are properly labeled, properly referenced in the auditor's report, and mathematically consistent with the basic statements.

## Presentation Completeness and Cleanliness

- Confirm all comparative period figures are present where required — a comparative presentation with a missing prior-year column, statement, or note section is a finding, not just a tie-out gap.

- Confirm currency, rounding level, and units are consistent throughout (e.g., whole dollars vs. thousands must not mix between statements, notes, and schedules without labeling).

- Confirm no draft markers, placeholder text, or square-bracket fill-ins remain anywhere in the document (e.g., "DRAFT", "[CLIENT]", "[DATE]", "XX,XXX", highlighted or tracked text).

## Unusual or Uncommon Items

- Flag anything in the document that is unusual or uncommon for a financial statement of the entity type under review and warrants specific attention by the reviewer. Examples (commercial): defined benefit pension plans (rare in private companies); derivative and hedging activity; foreign operations and translation; discontinued operations; impairment charges; business combinations completed during the year; equity method investments; VIE consolidations under the voting-interest model (as opposed to PCC scope exception); restricted cash or escrow arrangements; noncontrolling interests; redeemable equity instruments (ASC 480); SaaS or cloud computing arrangements (ASC 350-40); digital assets; any non-standard or industry-specific accounting treatment.
