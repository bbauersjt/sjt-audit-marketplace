# Final Proof Checklist (all frameworks)

1. Apply the judgment of an experienced audit preparer (commercial / nonprofit / governmental / EBP, as applicable) performing a final proof.
2. Goal: surface document-level issues a preparer would want to resolve before handing off to QC — not to conclude on engagement quality, firm risk, or release readiness.

## Internal Consistency of Narrative
1. If any management narrative, letter, or description accompanies the financial statements, confirm it is consistent with the presented results.
2. Flag any narrative claim inconsistent with the numbers.

## Opinion Appropriateness
1. Given the complete contents of the document, confirm the opinion type appears appropriate.
2. Check whether any matters disclosed in the footnotes (significant uncertainties, going concern indicators, material restatements, major subsequent events, departures from GAAP) would appear to require modification or emphasis in a currently unmodified report.

## Going Concern Section / Emphasis-of-Matter Consistency
1. If going concern language appears in the notes, confirm the auditor's report contains the corresponding Substantial Doubt section.
2. If a restatement, change in accounting principle, or significant subsequent event is disclosed, confirm the auditor's report contains an appropriate emphasis-of-matter paragraph.

## Sensitive Disclosures
1. Flag any disclosures that appear legally or reputationally sensitive that should be reviewed by engagement leadership before release.

## Cover Page and Transmittal
1. If a transmittal letter, cover page, or table of contents is present, confirm it is addressed correctly, dated correctly, and attributed appropriately.

## Supplementary Schedules
1. Confirm any supplementary schedules (consolidating, combining, operating expense detail) are properly labeled, properly referenced in the auditor's report, and mathematically consistent with the basic statements.

## Presentation Completeness and Cleanliness
1. Confirm all comparative period figures are present where required — a comparative presentation with a missing prior-year column, statement, or note section is a finding, not just a tie-out gap.
2. Confirm currency, rounding level, and units are consistent throughout (e.g., whole dollars vs. thousands must not mix between statements, notes, and schedules without labeling).
3. Confirm no draft markers, placeholder text, or square-bracket fill-ins remain anywhere in the document (e.g., "DRAFT", "[CLIENT]", "[DATE]", "XX,XXX", highlighted or tracked text).

## Unusual or Uncommon Items
1. Flag anything in the document that is unusual or uncommon for a financial statement of the entity type under review and warrants specific attention by the reviewer. Examples (commercial): defined benefit pension plans (rare in private companies); derivative and hedging activity; foreign operations and translation; discontinued operations; impairment charges; business combinations completed during the year; equity method investments; VIE consolidations under the voting-interest model (as opposed to PCC scope exception); restricted cash or escrow arrangements; noncontrolling interests; redeemable equity instruments (ASC 480); SaaS or cloud computing arrangements (ASC 350-40); digital assets; any non-standard or industry-specific accounting treatment.
