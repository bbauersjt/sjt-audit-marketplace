# EBP — Supplemental Schedules, Operational Compliance, Form 5500 Reconciliation

## Supplemental Schedules and Operational Compliance

### Schedule H, Line 4i — Schedule of Assets (Held at End of Year)

1. Confirm required header information: name of plan, EIN, three-digit plan number, plan year-end.
2. Confirm identity of issuer/borrower/lessor for each asset.
3. Confirm description (rate of interest, maturity date, collateral, par/maturity value where applicable).
4. Confirm cost (not required for participant-directed accounts; if omitted, note that fact).
5. Confirm current value.
6. Confirm party-in-interest assets are identified with an asterisk or other clear notation.
7. Confirm the schedule foots to the investment total on the face of the statement.

### Schedule H, Line 4j — Schedule of Reportable Transactions (Full Scope Only)

1. Required when a single transaction or series of transactions with the same person exceeded 5% of plan assets at BOY.
2. NOT required for Section 103(a)(3)(C) audits.
3. Required columns: identity of party, description of asset, purchase price, selling price, lease rental, expenses incurred, cost of asset, current value at transaction date, net gain/loss.
4. Confirm the 5% threshold calculation is correct (5% of BOY net assets, not EOY).

### Schedule H, Line 4a — Schedule of Delinquent Participant Contributions

1. Required if the plan had any late participant contribution remittances.
2. DOL position: deemed delinquent if not segregated as soon as reasonably possible (safe harbor: within 7 business days for plans with fewer than 100 participants; facts-and-circumstances for larger plans).
3. Required columns: total that constitute nonexempt prohibited transactions, broken into (a) contributions not corrected, (b) contributions corrected outside VFCP, (c) contributions pending correction in VFCP, (d) contributions fully corrected through VFCP and PTE 2002-51.
4. Confirm consistency with party-in-interest / prohibited transaction disclosure in the notes.
5. Verify lost earnings calculation methodology is referenced if disclosed.

### Schedule References in Auditor's Report

1. Confirm the auditor's "in relation to" paragraph identifies each supplemental schedule by exact title.
2. Confirm each schedule presented in the package is actually referenced in the report.
3. Confirm no schedule referenced in the report is missing from the package.

### Operational Compliance Red Flags

Review for indicators that should be reflected in the financial statements, supplemental schedules, or notes:

1. Late participant deferrals — any indication of late remittances should be reflected on Schedule of Delinquent Participant Contributions
2. Prohibited transactions — disclosed as such with corrective action
3. Definition of compensation issues — eligible comp vs. plan comp mismatches
4. Eligibility, vesting, distribution errors — typically disclosed if material and uncorrected
5. 402(g) excess deferrals, 415 limit excesses, ADP/ACP test failures — corrections within deadlines vs. operational failure disclosure
6. Forfeiture handling — balance disclosure, use during year
7. Loan defaults / deemed distributions
8. Self-correction (SCP), VCP, or VFCP corrections in process — proper subsequent event or contingency disclosure

## Form 5500 Reconciliation

If Form 5500 (or draft) is provided, perform the following:

1. Schedule H, Line 1l (Net Assets EOY) ↔ face of Statement of Net Assets
2. Schedule H, Line 1l (Net Assets BOY) ↔ prior year audited Net Assets EOY
3. Schedule H, Line 2k (Net Income/Loss) ↔ change in net assets per Statement of Changes
4. Reconciling items: typically accrued contributions receivable, benefits payable accrual — confirm proper disclosure in the notes via reconciliation table
5. Participant counts: BOY and EOY counts on Form 5500 reasonable in relation to plan size and any participant disclosures in the notes
6. Audit waiver / large plan filer status consistent with the audit report being attached

## Output additions

Beyond the standard output tabs, an EBP review adds the following tabs and tab content:

### Tab — Supplemental Schedules and Operational Compliance

1. All supplemental schedule and operational compliance procedures with Pass / Fail / Flag result and notes.
2. Sub-section for each supplemental schedule.
3. Sub-section for operational compliance red flags.

### Tab — Form 5500 Reconciliation

1. All Form 5500 reconciliation procedures with FS figure, Form 5500 figure, difference, reconciling item description, result.
2. If Form 5500 not provided, tab notes the limitation only.

### EBP-specific content within standard tabs

1. Engagement Profile tab: Plan type, audit scope (Section 103(a)(3)(C) vs. full), plan year-end, comparative period; plan sponsor, administrator, trustee, custodian, recordkeeper; EIN, plan number; plan size (large/small) and audit requirement basis; documents received vs. requested (limitations noted)
2. Report Language tab (SAS 136): procedures organized by report element, with Pass / Fail / Flag result and notes; two-part opinion check (if Section 103(a)(3)(C)) clearly broken out
3. Disclosure Review tab (ASC 962 / 960 / 965): procedures organized by standard/requirement with Pass / Fail / Flag result and notes; plan-type-specific procedures clearly labeled (DC vs. DB vs. H&W)
4. Cross-Reference tab: include a sub-section for logical and contextual consistency findings
