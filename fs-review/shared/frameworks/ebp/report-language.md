# EBP — Report Language and Standards Compliance (SAS 136 / AU-C 703)

Validate the auditor's report against SAS No. 136 (AU-C 703), the EBP-specific reporting standard. The form differs materially between Section 103(a)(3)(C) audits and full scope audits — apply the correct framework based on the plan type and audit scope identification.

## Common Elements (Both Scopes)

1. Title: "Independent Auditor's Report" or equivalent.
2. Addressee: plan administrator (or those charged with governance), NOT the plan sponsor's management.
3. Opinion section appears first, followed by Basis for Opinion.
4. Management's Responsibility: must explicitly reference the plan administrator's responsibility for (a) maintaining a current plan instrument including all amendments, (b) administering the plan, and (c) determining whether an ERISA Section 103(a)(3)(C) audit is permissible and whether the certification meets ERISA requirements (if applicable).
5. Auditor's Responsibility: description of audit conducted under GAAS.
6. Report on Supplemental Schedules — an "in relation to" paragraph identifying each ERISA-required supplemental schedule presented.
7. Signature (firm name), city and state, report date (no earlier than the date sufficient appropriate audit evidence was obtained).

## Section 103(a)(3)(C) Audit — Two-Part Opinion Structure

1. Confirm the opinion paragraph contains TWO opinions:
   - Whether the amounts and disclosures NOT covered by the certification are presented fairly in accordance with U.S. GAAP
   - Whether the investment information related to amounts certified by the qualified institution agrees with, or is derived from, the certification
2. Confirm the Basis for Opinion section explains the Section 103(a)(3)(C) limitation and that the auditor did not perform procedures on the certified investment information beyond comparing to the certification.
3. Confirm the Management's Responsibility section explicitly states the plan administrator's responsibility for determining the audit is permissible under ERISA Section 103(a)(3)(C) and that the certification meets ERISA requirements.
4. Confirm the report does NOT use the pre-SAS 136 "limited scope" language or disclaim an opinion — SAS 136 replaced the disclaimer with the two-part opinion structure for periods ending on or after December 15, 2021.

## Full Scope Audit

1. Confirm the standard SAS 134/136 unmodified opinion structure.
2. KAM (key audit matters) only if the engagement letter requires it — not standard for EBPs.

## Going Concern, Emphasis of Matter, Other Matter

1. Flag any going concern language and confirm proper disclosure in the notes.
2. Flag any emphasis-of-matter or other-matter paragraphs and confirm they are appropriate.

## Common Report Defects to Flag

1. Use of pre-SAS 136 "limited scope" or "disclaimer of opinion" language for a plan year ending after the SAS 136 effective date.
2. Addressing the report to the plan sponsor's management instead of the plan administrator.
3. Missing reference to Section 103(a)(3)(C) election in management's responsibility section.
4. Supplemental schedule reference paragraph missing or referring to schedules not actually presented.
5. Report date earlier than evidence date or signature date.
