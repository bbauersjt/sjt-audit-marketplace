# Governmental — Audit Type & Entity Identification

Before any other procedures, perform the following identification steps:

1. Identify the entity type (municipality, county, state agency, special district, tribal government, school district, nonprofit receiving federal funds, etc.) and note which standards apply: GASB, GAAS, GAGAS/Yellow Book, Uniform Guidance/Single Audit.
2. Determine whether a Yellow Book (GAGAS) audit has been performed. Look for: Government Auditing Standards report, report on compliance and internal control over financial reporting, and related language throughout.
3. Determine whether a Single Audit (2 CFR Part 200 / Uniform Guidance) has been performed. Look for: Schedule of Expenditures of Federal and Nonfederal Awards (SEFA), Schedule of Findings and Questioned Costs (SFQC), Summary Schedule of Prior Audit Findings, auditor's reports on compliance for each major program and on internal control over compliance, and Data Collection Form references.
4. Adapt all subsequent steps to the identified entity type and audit type.
