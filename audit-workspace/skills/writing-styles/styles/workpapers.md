# Workpaper Voice

## The reader
A reviewer, and a peer reviewer, who knows the client, GAAS, and the area cold. Document the work so they can re-perform it and reach your conclusion without asking questions and without reading anything they already know. Never explain a standard, a method, or a concept. State the parameters of this instance and stop. "Plan matches 50% up to 6%, 3% max," not how matching works.

## Default voice
Terse and factual: source, fact, impact, disposition. Figures to the cent, dates MM/DD/YYYY. Firm shorthand stays as written (w/o/e, NAE, NCN, PAJE, TM, PM, PAF, MLC, AJE, GL, TB, PY) and is never expanded. The shared baseline applies here first and hardest.

## Sourcing
"Per [source]" with an inline reference: per management, per the debt agreement, per check image, per the GL. Cross-reference with "See [index]" or "at [ref]." Reference tokens are illustrative; the format skill owns the actual indexing.

## Sections

Purpose. Infinitive, no subject. "To test [area] at 12/31/2025" for balances, "for the year ended 12/31/2025" for activity. Controls: "To test controls over [process]." Compliance: "To test controls over compliance for [program]" or "To test [X] covenant compliance." Combine clauses when one test covers balance and activity.

Scope. Fragment, no verb. Population plus threshold, threshold against TM (dollar in parens) and/or a PY percent. Coverage: "to achieve X% dollar coverage" plus a few small items from the untested population. Tiered subsequent thresholds escalate by period (Jan 1/3 TM, Feb 2/3, Mar and after full). Standard-driven sizes state the number ("25 participants to comply with DOL standards"). Defer with "See sampling form at [ref]." State the basis actually applied, not the decision rule (10% or 25, never "lesser of"). An immaterial area you would normally test gets "Note: [area] is immaterial, testing is NCN."

Procedure. First person plural, past tense, steps in sequence. "We obtained the following AP listing from [management], agreed it to the TB at [leadsheet], and performed a search for unrecorded liabilities." Describe only what this workpaper did. "The following" points to the embedded schedule.

Attributes (legend). Verb-first, no subject. "Agreed...," "Traced...," "Noted...," "Recalculated...". Source with per or to. Parenthetical to list the specific control elements checked ("(PO, sealed bids, council approval)"). w/o/e closes a passed attribute.

Notes. Two kinds, both terse, both sourced. (1) An exception or anything that breaks the expectation: state the issue, its effect on the balance or the audit, and the disposition (AJE, PAJE, MLC, finding, or a risk conclusion). (2) Information the reviewer needs so they do not flag a non-issue: "Note: per lease at [ref], no rent due in year one. Expense recognized straight line, no payments in 2025." Write no note for anything standard the reviewer could find on their own.

N/A descriptions. Three to five words, why the attribute does not apply. "Not a capital asset." "Participant is not eligible." "Not a payroll transaction." The attribute and the test already supply the context.

Conclusion. Default "appears reasonably stated." Use "is" only when 100% tested or an immaterial untested area.
- Balance or activity: "[area] appears reasonably stated at/for [date]."
- After an AJE: "After adjustment, [area] is reasonably stated at [date]."
- Completeness: "[area] appears to be complete at [date]."
- Compliance: "[entity] is in compliance with [X]."
- Subsequent events: "No unrecorded subsequent events noted."
- Controls passed: "Controls over [process] appear to be operating effectively."
- Controls failed: "Controls are ineffective. Control risk will be set at maximum. See finding."
- Walkthrough: "No deviations noted in the items selected for review." Does not assert operating effectiveness. You did not test enough to say that.

## Analytics
State the expectation, the actual, and "outside our expectation," then the cause and the conclusion. "Professional services increased $245,000, outside our expectation. Per the GL and management, [vendor] was contracted to manage the MIP to SAC transition, paid $235,000 in 2025, tested at [ref]. Net of this, change is within scope."

## What "done" looks like
The issue, its effect on the audit, and the disposition. Not a narration of the work. If a knowledgeable reviewer would skim past it as obvious, it does not belong on the page.
