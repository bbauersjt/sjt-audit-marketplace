# Progressive Subsequent

## Concept
Subsequent-period testing using a **tiered threshold** that increases over the months following year-end. A general completeness testing method.

Typical applications:
- **AP completeness** — used in all engagements (commercial, nonprofit, government, EBP — wherever unrecorded liabilities are a risk)
- **AR completeness** — used for nonprofits to test for unrecorded grants / contributions receivable. The AR sample MD will declare this when applicable

The tiering reflects the assumption that misstatements far removed from year-end are less likely to relate to the year-end balance, so the threshold can loosen as the period extends.

## Size determination (threshold tiering)
- **Month 1 after year-end**: threshold = **TM ÷ 3**
- **Month 2 after year-end**: threshold = **2 × TM ÷ 3**
- **Month 3 after year-end and beyond**: threshold = **TM**

n is the count of subsequent-period transactions whose amount exceeds the applicable monthly threshold.

## When to use
- AP completeness in any engagement — search for unrecorded liabilities at year-end
- AR completeness for nonprofits — search for unrecorded grants / contributions receivable at year-end
- Any subsequent-period completeness procedure where tiered materiality is appropriate

## Mode B recommendation
When proposing samples for an engagement, recommend `progressive-subsequent` for AR if the TB shows grants receivable, contributions receivable, or grant revenue accounts — that's the strongest signal it's a nonprofit-style population needing this method.

## When NOT to use
- Standard substantive testing of recorded balances (use 60% / TM / sampling form)
- Compliance / controls objectives

## Population assumptions
- Population is subsequent-period activity (cash receipts for AR completeness; cash disbursements / new invoices for A
