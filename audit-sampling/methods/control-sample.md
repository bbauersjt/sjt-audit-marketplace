# Control Sample

## Concept
Sample for testing operating effectiveness of internal controls. n is driven by control frequency, tolerable deviation rate, and reliance level.

## Size determination
- **Population ≥ 250** occurrences: n = **25**
- **Population < 250**: n = **ceil(population × 10%)**

Population is the number of times the control operated during the period (not dollars). For controls operating less frequently (quarterly, annually), the 10% rule yields the natural small sample (e.g., quarterly → 1, annually → 1).

## When to use
- Tests of controls in support of control reliance
- When the audit approach plans to reduce substantive procedures based on control effectiveness

## When NOT to use
- Substantive dollar testing
- Compliance testing where the requirement is regulatory rather than control-driven (use compliance-sample, or combined if the same selection serves both)

## Population assumptions
- Population is the set of times the control operated during the period
- Sampling unit is one operation
